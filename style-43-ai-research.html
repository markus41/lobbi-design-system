NeurIPS 2024 Transformers Attention

Sparse Attention Mechanisms for Efficient Large Language Models

Zhang, L., Kumar, R., Chen, M., et al.

We introduce a novel sparse attention mechanism that reduces computational complexity from O(n²) to O(n log n) while maintaining 99.4% of dense attention performance. Our approach enables training models with 2× longer context windows using equivalent computational resources.

ICML 2024 Reinforcement Learning Alignment

Constitutional AI: Harmlessness from AI Feedback

Anderson, T., Park, J., Williams, S., et al.

This work presents a scalable approach to training helpful, harmless, and honest AI systems using a constitution of principles rather than human feedback alone. We achieve state-of-the-art results on safety benchmarks while maintaining task performance.

Unified Vision-Language Pre-training with Contrastive Learning

Liu, X., Thompson, K., Martinez, E., et al.

We present a unified framework for vision-language pre-training that achieves state-of-the-art performance on 12 downstream tasks including image captioning, VQA, and visual reasoning. Our model demonstrates strong zero-shot transfer capabilities.
